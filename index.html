Free Market Capitalism: Why it Works

My Story

When I was nine, I started my own landscaping company, working during the summers and after school during the school year. In my first year, I earned upwards of $20 an hour, and made over $1,500. Since then, I have grown my company to around $30,000 in sales, and have learned a great deal about business. I have become a responsible person that understands the value of a dollar and has a good work ethic. This is a great example of why government interference in business is never a good idea.

Government Interference

Because the folks that run the government rarely know anything about economics, any time the government creates laws that hinder free market practices, inefficiencies occur. Imagine a "ladder of success." Every law that lawmakers create that hinders free market principles eliminates some of the bottom rungs of the ladder.
